About Me

Hello! My name is Farhan Hasin Fahim and I enjoy exploring the intersection of hardware and connectivity. My journey into the world of engineering began with a deep curiosity about how signals travel and how we can use technology to bridge gaps. Fast-forward to today, and I’ve had the privilege of working on diverse projects—from developing a Voice and Gesture Controlled Wheelchair in the ETE labs at RUET to digitalizing local history through the Chilahati Archive. My focus these days is mastering the architecture of modern communication networks and finding innovative ways to solve real-world problems.

Fast-forward to today, and I’ve had the privilege of serving as the President at Shopnotory Foundation, collaborating on electronics based engineering projects in the ETE labs at RUET. My main focus these days is mastering communication network architectures and developing impactful solutions like my Voice and Gesture Controlled Wheelchair.

I also recently initiated and launched Chilahati.com, a digital archive project dedicated to preserving the rich history and oral traditions of my hometown. My role involved everything from conceptualizing the archive to building the platform for naming origins and historical documentation.
